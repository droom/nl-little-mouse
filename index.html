Initial thoughts & exploration

Discovery session

A 'Discovery' session is just a meeting which enables me to listen to your ideas and goals, which mainly involves me asking lots of questions. Previous sessions have lasted anything from 60mins to 2 days (with breaks!) depending on the scale of the project. I've always found these to be generative and relaxed. The most important thing for me in these sessions is making sure everyone is heard, expertise is respected, and the problem(s) are thoroughly understood before attempting to find solutions.

  1. Understanding the company
    • Brand development often happens at 'strategic inflection points'. It would be great to know what plans Little Mouse has for the future, including markets into which to expand, product ranges, the make-up of the team, etc.
  2. Understanding the audience(s)
    • To make sure I understand who we're speaking to, and who we'd like to speak to, audience profiles would be good to create a this stage.
    • Understanding audiences/ Audience Profiles / Persona creation
  3. Understanding the market terrain
    • Competitor reviews. It would be ideal to compile a list of competitors to review beforehand, to then discuss what we think is working (and not working) with their direction.
  4. Requirements gathering
    • Where will the logo need to work?
    • Your preferred design software when working with templates
    • Define deliverables + set objectives/outcomes
  5. Concept generation / sketching

Working together

  • Keeping things documented
    • I like to keep a design document for every project, mainly to serve as a record of my thinking, and to make a note of decisions made along the way. These also house ideas to potentially explore at a later date.
  • Collaborative and open communication
    • Co-create an inspiration board: This could include menus, merchandise, interiors
    • I work with Figma which has a feedback tool built-in.
  • Applying Agile to the design process
    • Agile methodology is often used in development cycles, but I've found some aspects work really well for design work. Working in sprints, with structured feedback windows and crits conducting ‘retrospectives’ at the end of the project (optional, of course).
  • Working transparently + openly
    • Where possible, show techniques + process to develop into case studies for the design education element of Norwood Letters. Provide rationales and explain decisions.
  • Working with the local community
    • If budget and scope allow, collaborate with local specialists (Photographers, Illustrators, Sign painters, Sign makers, printers)
  • Working with development teams (Gareth!)
    • For digital products, I create 'style tiles’ to communicate web patterns.

Pricing

What I’m chiefly interested in doing is developing long term relationships with local businesses and the wider community, and this is only possible if built on a foundation of respect and understanding. Essentially, I need to understand the financial contraints that Little Mouse is working under to suggest a fair price, and I think I'll be in a better position to do this after meeting with you both. The scale of the work to be delivered can also be negotiated to fit your budget.

As mentioned previously, you'd be helping Norwood Letters get off the ground, and develop client processes, so I'll be sure to factor that in.
